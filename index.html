Natural Disaster Impact on Unemployment Rates in Segmented Countries

Every year, hundreds of natural disasters occur across the globe. Earthquakes, tsunamis, storms, droughts, wildfires, floods, epidemics, landslides and volcanic activity affect hundreds of economic structures throughout the world, yet some countries are economically stronger than others. Natural disasters do not occur based off of the resilience or development status of a country, and no one can control when or where these disasters may strike. Therefore, the economic recovery periods of a country may vary based on both economic development and the severity of the natural disaster.
